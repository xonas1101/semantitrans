IDIOM DETECTOR: FIGURATIVE vs LITERAL CLASSIFICATION (train_idiom_detector.py)
==============================================================================
Plot: detector_cm.png (confusion matrix on held-out MAGPIE data)

A DistilBERT classifier fine-tuned by us on the MAGPIE corpus decides whether
a matched idiom is used FIGURATIVELY ("kicked the bucket" = died) or LITERALLY
("kicked the bucket across the yard"). Only figurative uses get their meaning
substituted before translation — this gating prevents the system from
"correcting" idioms that were meant literally.

  Held-out accuracy : 0.965
  F1 (figurative)   : 0.976

Gating rule: substitution is suppressed only when p(literal) >= 0.70, since
idioms are figurative by default (raw argmax was too conservative on
borderline cases).

Companion plot: testset.png — composition of our evaluation set
(250 TTS clips: 200 figurative + 50 literal idiom usages, all covered by the
gloss knowledge base).
